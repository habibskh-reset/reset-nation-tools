Layer 1

Foundation Layer

Food first. Powders only fill the gaps.

Core Supplements

Whey Protein Convenient way to complete protein intake.
Mass Gainer Easy calorie increase for people struggling to eat enough.
Casein Protein Slow digesting protein for long gaps between meals.
Plant Protein Alternative option for lactose intolerance or vegan diets.
Meal Replacement Convenience option when meals are inconsistent.

Key Idea

  • • Hit daily protein targets first.
  • • Consistency beats powders.
  • • Don't out-supplement a bad diet.
Layer Reminder "Supplements can’t replace poor nutrition."
Layer 2

Support Layer

You can't perform on deficiencies.

Vitamins

Vitamin D Supports recovery and overall body functioning.
Vitamin B12 Useful for energy support and deficiency correction.
Multivitamin Basic nutritional backup. Not a replacement for food.
Vitamin C Supports recovery and immunity.

Minerals

Iron Important when deficiency affects energy and performance.
Magnesium Supports sleep, recovery, and muscle function.
Zinc Supports general recovery and body functions.
Calcium Supports bone health when intake is low.

Recovery Support

Omega 3 Supports recovery and overall health.
Electrolytes Useful during dehydration and heavy sweating.

Key Idea

  • • Support the system, don't just stimulate it.
  • • Get bloodwork to find true gaps.
Layer Reminder "Foundation should already be stable before fixing support gaps."
Layer 3

Performance Layer

These amplify performance. They don't create it.
Weak basics = weak results.

Core Supplements

Creatine Monohydrate Supports strength and training performance.
Pre Workout Improves workout alertness and intensity feeling.
Caffeine Temporary focus and stimulation support.
Beta Alanine May help reduce high-intensity workout fatigue.
Citrulline Malate Supports blood flow and workout performance feel.

Key Idea

  • • Wait until training is consistent.
  • • Don't use stimulants to mask bad sleep.
  • • Expect 5% improvement, not 50%.
Layer Reminder "Fix Foundation and Support layers first."
Layer 4

Situational Layer

Highly specific tools for specific problems.
Not essential for the average lifter.

Core Supplements

BCAA Mostly useful when protein intake is low.
EAA Amino acid support in low-protein situations.
Glutamine May help recovery and gut stress in high training volume.
L-Carnitine Can slightly support fat metabolism. Not a shortcut.
L-Arginine Supports temporary pump and blood flow.
Intra Workout Useful during long or intense training sessions.
Pump Products Improve temporary pump and workout feel.
Ashwagandha May help stress and recovery in some people.

Key Idea

  • • Only buy if solving a diagnosed issue.
  • • Don't buy just for the "advanced" feeling.
Layer Reminder "Only consider these when lower layers are already consistent."
Layer 5

Hype Layer

Selling shortcuts to people with weak foundations.

Core Supplements

Fat Burners Cannot replace calorie deficit.
Test Boosters Often marketed beyond realistic impact.
Detox Products Mostly marketing-driven cleansing claims.
Extreme Transformation Products Sell unrealistic shortcut expectations.
Anabolic Muscle Builders Marketing-heavy muscle gain positioning.

Key Idea

  • • Names are designed to manipulate psychology.
  • • No pill outworks a bad diet.
  • • Save your money for Layer 1.
Layer Reminder "If lower layers are broken, this layer becomes mostly marketing."

The Pyramid Rule

Never spend money on an upper layer while the lower is broken.

Broken sleep → pre-workout won't fix it.
Low protein → creatine won't save it.
No calorie deficit → fat burner won't help.

Self-Audit

Before Buying A Supplement

  • Protein target hit?
  • Sleep schedule stable?
  • Training consistently?
  • Diagnosed deficiency?
  • Solving a real problem?
  • Or just chasing hype?
